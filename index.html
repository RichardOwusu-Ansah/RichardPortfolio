About Me

I am Richard Owusu-Ansah, a Geographer, Geospatial Data Scientist, and Researcher currently pursuing a Master of Science in Geography and Environment at The George Washington University. My research focuses on environmental predictive modeling, urban heat islands, carbon dynamics, and flood risk assessment, utilizing GIS and geospatial programming to analyze environmental challenges and inform sustainable solutions.

With expertise in Python, ArcGIS, QGIS, and geospatial analysis, I have worked on projects exploring land cover change, illegal mining impacts, and climate change adaptation strategies. I am particularly interested in using spatial models to better understand the interactions between urban environments, carbon emissions, and climate risks, aiming to enhance urban resilience to environmental stressors.

Feel free to explore my work below, and feel free to contact me if you're interested in collaboration or further discussion.
